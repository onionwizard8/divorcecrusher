By the time you finish this article, you’ll know exactly how to expose parental alienation and win full custody.

HOOK

You’re a father who’s been through hell. Your ex-wife has been poisoning your children’s minds against you. She’s telling them you’re a monster, a liar, a bad guy. But you know the truth. You’re a good dad, and you want to be there for your kids. You’re not a monster. You’re their dad. And you’re sick of the lies.

THE SETUP

Parental alienation is one of the most insidious tactics ex-wives use to sabotage a father’s relationship with his children. It’s a form of emotional abuse that turns your children against you, making you look like the villain in their eyes. The courts often side with the mother, assuming that the child’s negative feelings towards you are justified. But what if you could prove that the mother’s allegations are nothing but lies? What if you could show the court that you’re the victim of parental alienation? You can. And you can walk out of court with full custody.

THE CASE LAW

Case 1: Lemay v. Board of the College of Midwives

In this case, Gloria Lemay was convicted of criminal contempt for disobeying an order from the court. While this case doesn’t directly deal with parental alienation, it highlights the importance of following court orders. If your ex-wife is disobeying orders and using them to alienate your children, you can use this case to argue that her actions are not just harmful to you, but also illegal.

Case 2: Lotzkar v. Lotzkar

In this case, Marian Brenda Lotzkar and Bernard Sydney Lotzkar were divorced after a 42-year marriage. While the case primarily deals with the division of assets, it also touches on issues of custody and access. In such cases, the courts often look at the best interests of the child. If you can show that your ex-wife’s actions are harming your children’s best interests, you can use this case to argue for full custody.

THE WEAPON

1. Prove Parental Alienation

Parental alienation is a serious issue that can have long-lasting effects on your children. You need to prove that your ex-wife is actively working to turn your children against you. This means gathering evidence such as emails, text messages, and witness statements. You can use the following legal provisions to support your case:

  • Family Law Act, Section 37(1): This section outlines the factors the court considers when making decisions about the best interests of the child. One of these factors is the role each parent has played and continues to play in the child’s life. If your ex-wife is actively working to alienate your children from you, this can be a significant factor in the court’s decision.

  • Divorce Act, Section 16(10): This section states that the court must consider the child’s physical and emotional safety, security, and well-being. If your ex-wife’s actions are harming your children’s emotional well-being, you can use this section to argue for full custody.

2. Use Case Law to Your Advantage

When you’re in court, you need to be armed with case law that supports your argument. Use the cases mentioned above to show the court that your ex-wife’s actions are not just harmful to you, but also illegal. You can use the following language:

  • “In Lemay v. Board of the College of Midwives, the court found that disobeying court orders is not just harmful to the person who is the victim, but also illegal. My ex-wife is disobeying orders and using them to alienate my children from me. This is not just harmful to me, but also illegal.”
  • “In Lotzkar v. Lotzkar, the court considered the best interests of the child when making decisions about custody and access. My ex-wife’s actions are harming my children’s best interests. I should have full custody to protect them from further harm.”

THE TAKEAWAY

  1. Gather Evidence: Collect emails, text messages, and witness statements to prove that your ex-wife is actively working to turn your children against you.
  2. Use Legal Provisions: Use Family Law Act, Section 37(1) and Divorce Act, Section 16(10) to argue that your ex-wife’s actions are harming your children’s best interests.
  3. Use Case Law: Use the cases mentioned above to show the court that your ex-wife’s actions are not just harmful to you, but also illegal.
  4. Be Aggressive: Don’t be afraid to push back against your ex-wife’s tactics. You’re fighting for your children’s best interests, and you need to be aggressive to win.
